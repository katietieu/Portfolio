Human-Computer Interactions

CPSC 581: Human-Computer Interaction II is the advanced HCI course at the University of Calgary. I took it in winter 2020.

In it, I practiced techniques for designing interactive human-computer interfaces. The course consisted of four projects, created respectively using Microsoft Visual Studio 2017, Android Studio, Arduino, and HTML/CSS/JavaScript.

This class emphasized creative thinking while pushing for more practical outcomes. It stressed the design process: sketching, critiquing and refining, and then implementing. The task:

Sketch, design, and implement a software application where the central interaction is with a button, such that the interaction represents some aspect of your client's personality or character.

Our client:

Teemo, The Swift Scout

League of Legends is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where players control characters called champions, each having unique abilities. Teemo is a champion who uses poison darts and mushrooms.

Within the community, Teemo has a reputation of being unbearably annoying. He is often jokingly characterized as a devil, which the developer Riot Games humoured by creating the Little Devil Teemo skin. As an opponent, Teemo is stealthy and hard to catch, and has many crowd control abilities which debilitate his enemies. Our final application highlights this elusiveness and hindrance, and is overall intended to irritate the user with a humourous, devilish twist added at the end.

Final design:

Our final application shows Teemo on Summoner's Rift, the map on which most League games are played. There is a health and mana bar at the bottom of the screen, mimicking the in-game UI. As you move your mouse to click Teemo, you'll find that he giggles and runs away. As you chase him, you may run into his invisible poison mushrooms, which then explode and cause you to lose HP. The more you chase him, the more he laughs, and the more mushrooms you run over. When you eventually run out of HP and "die", Devil Teemo appears and slowly takes over your screen with distorted laughter.

You have been slain.
